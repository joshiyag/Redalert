Surveillance

One crucial aspect of any security system is surveillance. This involves the use of cameras and other monitoring devices to observe and record activities in and around the protected area.Surveillance helps deter potential criminals, provides evidence in case of incidents, and allows for real-time monitoring of the premises.

Access Control

Access control is another vital point in a security system. It involves managing and regulating who can enter or exit a specific area.This can be achieved through various means, such as physical barriers like locks and gates, as well as electronic systems like keycards, biometric readers (fingerprint or iris scanners), or PIN codes

Alarm and Intrusion Detection

An effective security system should have robust alarm and intrusion detection mechanisms. These systems are designed to detect and alert authorities or designated personnel when unauthorized access or suspicious activities occur.
